## Foundations & References

- [Physics Stack Exchange](https://physics.stackexchange.com/) – Community-driven Q&A for physics theory, problems, and applications.
- [NIST Physical Constants](https://physics.nist.gov/constants) – Authoritative reference for fundamental physical constants.
- [arXiv Physics](https://arxiv.org/archive/physics) – Open-access repository for physics preprints.
- [Encyclopedia of Physics](https://plato.stanford.edu/) – Scholarly reference articles relevant to physical sciences.
- [CODATA](https://www.codata.org/) – Internationally recommended values of physical constants.

## Classical Physics

- [Classical Mechanics (Goldstein)](https://www.amazon.com/Classical-Mechanics-Herbert-Goldstein/dp/0201657023) – Foundational reference for Newtonian and Lagrangian mechanics.
- [MIT OpenCourseWare – Classical Mechanics](https://ocw.mit.edu/) – University-level lectures and notes on mechanics.
- [Fluid Mechanics Resources](https://nptel.ac.in/) – Learning materials covering fluid dynamics and hydrodynamics.
- [OpenFOAM](https://www.openfoam.com/) – Open-source CFD software for fluid mechanics simulations.
- [LAMMPS](https://www.lammps.org/) – Molecular dynamics simulator widely used in classical physics research.

## Modern & Theoretical Physics

- [Theoretical Physics Reference](https://arxiv.org/) – Preprints covering advanced theoretical models and frameworks.
- [General Relativity Living Reviews](https://link.springer.com/journal/41114) – In-depth reviews of general relativity research.
- [Einstein Online](https://www.einstein-online.info/) – Educational portal on relativity and modern physics.
- [SymPy Physics](https://docs.sympy.org/latest/modules/physics/index.html) – Symbolic mathematics tools for theoretical physics.

## Quantum Physics

- [Quantum Mechanics MIT OCW](https://ocw.mit.edu/) – Lecture notes and problem sets on quantum theory.
- [QuTiP](https://qutip.org/) – Open-source Python framework for simulating quantum systems.
- [Qiskit](https://qiskit.org/) – Open-source SDK for quantum computing and quantum information.
- [Cirq](https://quantumai.google/cirq) – Python library for quantum circuits and algorithms.
- [Quantum Open Source Foundation](https://qosf.org/) – Community-driven quantum software and education.

## Astrophysics & Cosmology

- [NASA Astrophysics Data System](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/) – Research database for astronomy and astrophysics papers.
- [ESA Science](https://www.cosmos.esa.int/) – European Space Agency datasets and mission science.
- [Astropy](https://www.astropy.org/) – Python library for astronomy and astrophysics computations.
- [CosmoMC](https://cosmologist.info/cosmomc/) – Markov Chain Monte Carlo engine for cosmological parameter estimation.
- [HEASARC](https://heasarc.gsfc.nasa.gov/) – High-energy astrophysics data archive.

## Condensed Matter & Materials Science

- [Materials Project](https://materialsproject.org/) – Open database of material properties from ab initio calculations.
- [Quantum ESPRESSO](https://www.quantum-espresso.org/) – Integrated suite for electronic-structure calculations.
- [VASP](https://www.vasp.at/) – First-principles simulation package for materials modeling.
- [Wannier90](https://wannier.org/) – Tool for electronic structure and Wannier functions.
- [AFLOW](https://aflow.org/) – Automatic framework for materials discovery and design.

## Nuclear & Particle Physics

- [CERN Open Data Portal](https://opendata.cern.ch/) – Public datasets from LHC experiments.
- [Particle Data Group](https://pdg.lbl.gov/) – Definitive resource for particle physics properties.
- [ROOT](https://root.cern/) – Data analysis framework used in high-energy physics.
- [GEANT4](https://geant4.web.cern.ch/) – Toolkit for simulating particle interactions with matter.
- [INSPIRE-HEP](https://inspirehep.net/) – Literature database for high-energy physics.

## Computational Physics

- [NumPy](https://numpy.org/) – Core numerical computing library for physics simulations.
- [SciPy](https://scipy.org/) – Scientific computing tools for optimization, integration, and signal processing.
- [Julia Physics Ecosystem](https://juliaphysics.github.io/) – High-performance physics packages written in Julia.
- [MATLAB](https://www.mathworks.com/products/matlab.html) – Numerical computing environment widely used in physics.
- [PETSc](https://petsc.org/) – Scalable solvers for large-scale scientific computation.

## Simulation & Modeling Tools

- [COMSOL Multiphysics](https://www.comsol.com/) – Finite element simulation platform for physics-based modeling.
- [Simulink](https://www.mathworks.com/products/simulink.html) – Model-based design for dynamic systems.
- [FEniCS](https://fenicsproject.org/) – Open-source computing platform for PDE-based simulations.
- [MOOSE Framework](https://mooseframework.inl.gov/) – Multiphysics simulation framework.
- [Elmer FEM](https://www.csc.fi/web/elmer) – Finite element software for multiphysical problems.

## Data, Repositories & Open Science

- [Zenodo](https://zenodo.org/) – Open repository for scientific data and publications.
- [Figshare](https://figshare.com/) – Research data sharing platform.
- [Open Science Framework](https://osf.io/) – Collaboration and data management for scientific research.
- [Dryad](https://datadryad.org/) – Curated repository for scientific datasets.
- [PANGAEA](https://www.pangaea.de/) – Earth and environmental science data archive.

## Learning Resources

### Tutorials
- [MIT OpenCourseWare Physics](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/physics/) – Free university-level physics courses.
- [Khan Academy Physics](https://www.khanacademy.org/science/physics) – Beginner-friendly physics tutorials.
- [Physics LibreTexts](https://phys.libretexts.org/) – Open-access physics textbooks and learning modules.

### Guides
- [HyperPhysics](http://hyperphysics.phy-astr.gsu.edu/) – Conceptual physics reference with visual explanations.
- [The Feynman Lectures](https://www.feynmanlectures.caltech.edu/) – Classic lectures on physics fundamentals.
- [Physics Today](https://physicstoday.scitation.org/) – News and analysis in modern physics research.

### Courses
- *Classical Mechanics* – Undergraduate-level mechanics curriculum.
- *Quantum Mechanics* – Core course covering wave mechanics and operators.
- *Statistical Mechanics* – Course on thermodynamics and ensemble theory.
